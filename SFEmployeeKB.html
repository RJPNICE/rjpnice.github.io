Disciplinary Process

When to use

Use the disciplinary process for concerns about conduct, performance, or policy breaches. Key stages: informal resolution, investigation, formal hearing, and outcome.

  • Informal Resolution — try to resolve issues early through conversation.
  • Investigation — fact-finding to ensure fair consideration.
  • Formal Hearing — colleague can respond to evidence.
  • Outcome — ranging from no action to formal written warnings.

Grievance Process

If you feel you've been treated unfairly

Start with an informal discussion with your manager where possible. If unresolved, submit a formal Grievance Form. We will investigate and provide an outcome letter.

  1. Informal Discussion with manager
  2. Submit Grievance Form
  3. Investigation & meetings
  4. Outcome letter

Appeals

If you disagree with a decision

Submit an Appeal Form within 5 working days of receiving the outcome. Appeals are reviewed by a different manager/panel and decisions are issued in writing.
